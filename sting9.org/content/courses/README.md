## Remaining Individuals Chapters - Quick Reference

### Chapter 6: Package Delivery Scams
- 174% increase in delivery scams
- USPS, FedEx, UPS, Amazon impersonation
- Fake redelivery fees ($1.99 typical)
- Red flags: Unexpected notices, shortened URLs, payment requests
- Verify through official apps/websites only

### Chapter 7: Romance Scams
- 50% start on Facebook/Instagram/Snapchat
- Average losses higher than other scam types
- "Pig butchering" - romance + crypto investment
- Red flags: Quick love declarations, can't meet, money requests
- Never send money to online-only relationships

### Chapter 8: Tax and IRS Scams
- $9.1 billion in tax fraud (2024)
- $8,199 average loss per person
- IRS never initiates contact via email/text/social
- Red flags: Urgent refund claims, arrest threats, gift card payments
- Real IRS only contacts via mail initially

### Chapter 9: Cryptocurrency and Investment Scams
- $9.32 billion lost to crypto scams (2024)
- $5.81 billion to investment scams
- "Pig butchering": $3.2 billion
- Red flags: Guaranteed returns, urgency, celebrity endorsements
- Crypto transactions are irreversible

### Chapter 10: Online Shopping and E-Commerce Scams
- BogusBazaar: 75,000 fake websites
- 44% of social media fraud = shopping
- Red flags: 70-90% discounts, new domains, no reviews
- Verify: Domain age, reviews, return policy, SSL certificate
- Use credit cards for better protection

### Chapter 11: Recognizing Psychological Manipulation
- Urgency, fear, authority, trust, greed tactics
- Why elderly are targeted (cognitive + savings)
- Emotional triggers bypass rational thinking
- Build defenses: Pause, verify, consult others
- Training reduces success by 86%

### Chapter 12: What To Do If Targeted or Compromised
- Immediate: Disconnect internet, don't delete evidence
- Financial: Call bank, freeze cards, dispute charges
- Passwords: Change all, enable 2FA
- Identity: Credit freeze, fraud alerts, monitor reports
- Report: FTC, FBI IC3, company fraud departments
- Recovery: Most wire transfers unrecoverable, act fast

## Remaining Professionals Chapters - Quick Reference

### Chapter 2: CEO Fraud and Whaling Attacks
- 442% surge in vishing
- 42x more QR phishing to C-suite
- Deepfake cases: Arup $25-39M, Hong Kong CFO $25M
- Voice cloning: 3 seconds = 85% match

### Chapter 3: Wire Transfer and Invoice Fraud
- 90% of companies targeted
- 63% experienced wire fraud
- 60% lost >$5M (136% increase)
- VEC increased 66% in H1 2024
- Prevention: Multi-person approval, verification calls

### Chapter 4: Vendor Email Compromise (VEC)
- 69% of companies targeted
- 137% increase in financial services
- Supply chain attacks up 78%
- Red flags: Payment detail changes, urgent requests
- Verify all vendor changes via known contacts

### Chapter 5: Payroll Diversion Scams
- 815% increase in diversion attempts
- Proofpoint blocked 35,000 scams
- Targets: HR/payroll departments
- Prevention: In-person verification, waiting periods
- Test transactions before full paychecks

### Chapter 6: M365 / Google Workspace Attacks
- 35% of phishing impersonates Microsoft
- 71.4% of M365 users have compromised account monthly
- 10x surge in password attacks (30B/month)
- QR code phishing to C-suite
- Security: Phishing-resistant MFA, conditional access, monitoring

### Chapter 7: Supply Chain and Third-Party Risks
- 78% increase in supply chain attacks
- Major 2024: Change Healthcare, CrowdStrike, CDK Global
- XZ Utils backdoor (2.5 year operation)

### Chapter 8: Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)
- 1 in 4 companies affected
- 43% of high-severity incidents
- Median 2 days to exfiltration
- Groups: Salt Typhoon, Lazarus, APT41, APT29/28
- Detection: Threat hunting, EDR, behavioral analytics

### Chapter 9: Deepfakes and AI-Enhanced Attacks
- 194% surge in AI fraud
- 3,000% increase in deepfakes
- $1 trillion projected cost

- Detection: Challenge questions, out-of-band verification

### Chapter 10: Incident Response and Corporate Policies
- Phases: Preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery
- Policy elements: Acceptable use, email security, wire transfers
- Training requirements: Regular, role-specific, simulated
- Documentation: Procedures, approvals, escalation

### Chapter 11: Security Awareness Training Programs
- 86% improvement with training
- 34.3% untrained fail phishing → 4.6% after 1 year
- Weekly training = 96% improvement
- Components: Initial training, simulations, metrics, feedback
- Measure: Failure rates, reporting rates, time to detect

### Chapter 12: Building a Security-Conscious Organization
- Culture elements: Leadership, empowerment, blame-free reporting
- Metrics: Phishing results, incident response time, training completion
- Success factors: Executive sponsorship, resources, communication
- Continuous improvement: Assessments, testing, adaptation
- ROI: $1M average savings from training
